Designing RentEdge: Embedding RentSpree into the MLS Ecosystem

Leading the strategic design of a B2B2B product that repositions RentSpree as an integrated rental platform within MLS ecosystems. This work set the vision and visual language that drove partner pitch decks and shaped the product roadmap for 2025–2026.

The Challenge

A Fundamental Growth Constraint

RentSpree had built a strong screening product but adoption remained tied to the old model of partner opt-ins buried in listing flows. Only 20% of agents who opted in actually converted to active usage. The system required agents to leave their existing workflows, introducing friction at every step. To scale beyond incremental improvements, we needed to rethink the entire positioning: not as an add-on, but as an embedded, indispensable part of the MLS experience.

🔗

Low Adoption From Opt-ins

Only 4 of 5 opt-ins converted to active usage. Partner activation efforts yielded limited results because they were incremental, not transformational.

📍

Brand Awareness Gap

Agents in new partnerships had minimal awareness of RentSpree's value. Rentals remained seen as secondary to sales, not a core business opportunity.

🚪

Workflow Friction

Agents had to opt in through listing creation flows or jump to external systems. Every additional step reduced conversion and engagement.

🏠

MLS Vulnerability

MLSs needed to stay relevant by delivering member value. Rentals were underleveraged a strategic opportunity being left on the table.

The Strategic Insight

Beyond Screening Alone: Repositioning RentSpree in the MLS

The critical insight was this: incremental improvements won't get us to fundamentally better outcomes. Agents were avoiding rentals because the entire experience was fragmented they had to jump between systems, manage compliance risks, and had no visibility into market trends. MLSs, meanwhile, needed to position themselves as leaders, not laggards. The real opportunity wasn't to build a better feature; it was to reframe the entire value proposition.

RentEdge was designed as a modular ecosystem that would be embedded directly into MLS platforms, making it invisible that agents were even using RentSpree. Instead of "opt-in to screening," it became "your MLS just gave you powerful rental tools." This shifted the narrative from an add-on to an indispensable part of the agent's daily workflow.

"RentEdge brings rentals to the forefront of the MLS experience, giving agents simple, powerful tools and helping MLSs position themselves as the platform for all real estate today and into the future."

The Approach

From Design Summit to Product Reality

The design work for RentEdge was fundamentally different from consumer-facing product design. We needed to design for integration, not acquisition. This required a different methodology one focused on system thinking, white-label flexibility, and embedding into partner ecosystems.

Design Summit → Concepts → Partner Pitch Decks → PRDs → Execution
Strategic Design Process for B2B2B Platform Product
  • 1

    Design Summit (Q1 2025)

    Brought together product, design, engineering, and business teams for a two-day workshop. Rather than iterating on existing screens, we used this time to develop foundational system thinking around how rentals should appear and behave across the MLS experience. This cross-functional alignment was critical every team had to understand the modular strategy.

  • 2

    Strategic Product Concepts

    Developed three core MVPs: Screen (baseline tenant screening integrated into MLS), Insights (rental market data and benchmarks as widgets), and Distribute (syndication of listings to expand reach). Each module was designed to stand alone but also work as part of a larger platform, enabling MLSs to choose what made sense for their business.

  • 3

    Partner-Ready Pitch Decks

    Translated the design concepts into visual, compelling pitch decks that could be used directly in conversations with MLS partners. These decks showed the experience from an agent's perspective (how rentals would appear and behave within their daily workflow) and from an MLS perspective (how RentEdge positioned them as innovators).

  • 4

    Product Specifications

    Formalized the design concepts into detailed PRDs that included interaction patterns, white-label customization guidelines, SSO/integration flows, and data requirements. This ensured engineering could build to specification while maintaining flexibility for partner-specific customizations.

  • 5

    Ongoing Execution (2025–2026)

    As MVPs were built and deployed, continued to refine the design system based on real partner feedback. the design team led the day-to-day design execution, while I maintained the strategic vision and coordinated with business development and product leadership.

Integration Patterns & Design System

One of the most critical design decisions was establishing reusable integration patterns. RentEdge had to work with different MLS platforms (PrimeMLS uses Paragon, others use Flex, etc.), each with different technology stacks. The design system had to be flexible enough to work across these, but consistent enough that agents recognized RentEdge no matter where they accessed it.

White-Label Customization

Each MLS partner gets their own branded experience. The core components (buttons, cards, forms) remain consistent for UX coherence, but colors, logos, and co-branding elements change per partner. This required careful CSS variable strategy and flexible layout patterns.

SSO & Partner Dashboards

Agents needed seamless access to RentSpree through their MLS. Designed partner-specific SSO flows and dedicated RentSpree dashboards that highlight only the modules and content relevant to that MLS partnership.

Widget Architecture

MLS platforms have different constraints (height limits, loading patterns, responsive behavior). Designed widgets that could be embedded as iframes or native components, with careful attention to usability within partner UI constraints.

Data Synchronization

Designed clear data flows between MLS systems and RentSpree. This required documentation of what data flows where, how it syncs, privacy/compliance considerations, and what happens when sync fails.

Reflection

Lessons from Designing for Ecosystem Integration

RentEdge taught me critical lessons about designing for B2B2B products and ecosystem integration that differ fundamentally from consumer or direct B2B design.

Design for Invisibility, Not Delight
In consumer design, we celebrate our brand and create moments of delight. In ecosystem design, the best outcome is when users don't notice they're using our product they just see the partner's interface. This requires a different mindset: restraint over expression, systems over stories.
Strategic Design Shapes Business Outcomes
The design work didn't just inform product it directly influenced partner conversations and business development strategy. Early, clear concepts gave sales teams something tangible to show partners, reducing risk and accelerating deal closure.
Modularity Is a Design Problem, Not Just Engineering
True modularity requires thoughtful design. Every component had to work standalone and as part of a system. This meant designing decision trees for which modules to activate, how they interact, and how they degrade gracefully when partners opt out.
Partner Constraints Are Design Opportunities
Each MLS platform had different technology constraints (height limits, responsive behavior, data architecture). Rather than fighting these, we designed around them. This led to more creative solutions and stronger, more flexible components.
Cross-functional Alignment Happens at the Summit
The design summit was essential. Synchronous, in-person time with product, engineering, and business teams ensured everyone understood the strategy. This alignment made execution faster and reduced the need for constant back-and-forth.
Go Deep on First Principles
Rather than redesigning the existing screening flow, we asked: "What if rentals were positioned as core to the agent's workflow, not an add-on?" This question led to a completely different approach that resonated with partners and agents.

"Incremental improvements won't get us to fundamentally better outcomes. To truly move the needle, we need to take a different approach to how we solve the problem."

Core insight that led to RentEdge strategy
