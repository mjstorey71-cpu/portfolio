The Challenge

An Aging Experience at Scale

RentSpree's rental application platform was critical to the business the single highest-leverage product moment for conversion. But the legacy experience showed its age. Applicants faced a fragmented, repetitive flow with multiple friction points, unclear progress indicators, and mobile experiences that didn't optimize for today's user behaviors.

📊

Stalled Conversion Metrics

The application completion funnel had plateaued, with no clear path to improvement without a fundamental redesign of the experience.

📱

Legacy Code Burden

The existing codebase was difficult to maintain and extend, slowing iteration and making small changes risky across the product.

🔄

Fragmented User Journey

Applicants repeated information entry, navigated confusing section transitions, and lacked clarity on their progress through the flow.

📈

Opportunity Cost

A 7% lift in start-to-paid conversion unlocked $1.5M in annual revenue, requiring bold structural changes to the application flow.

The Strategy

Design-Ahead-of-Sprints Leadership

Rather than allow engineering sprints to dictate design direction, I established a design-led methodology where the product design team worked weeks ahead of development. This gave us time to discover, test assumptions, and iterate before code began unlocking faster shipping and higher-quality outcomes.

As Head of Product Design and Research, I bridged product and engineering, ensuring design leadership was embedded in roadmap planning. our design lead, led day-to-day execution while I provided strategic direction and stakeholder alignment.

"We set a clear north star for applicant experience improvements, and started the design team on the path to getting ahead of the tech sprints, allowing for more time to iterate and discover."

Michael Storey, Head of Product Design and Research
Design Pillars
📍
Mobile-First Design
Optimize for the device where most applicants complete forms mobile phones with variable network conditions.
📝
Linearized Flow
Replace scattered chapters with a clear, step-by-step progression. Clear progress indicators reduce cognitive load.
🧩
Reduced Repetition
Collect personal information once at the start, reuse it throughout the flow. No re-asking for the same data.
✅
Summary & Review
Let applicants review and edit all entered information before submission. Build confidence in data accuracy.

Launch & Iteration

Soft Launch to Scaled Testing

We launched Phase 1 on December 17, 2025, starting with a soft launch of 50–100 new applicants per day. This allowed us to catch showstopper bugs, monitor early conversion signals, and learn before dialing up to full traffic.

🎯
December 2–15, 2025
UAT & Final Testing
Completed user acceptance testing with Customer Ops and internal stakeholders. Fixed P0 bugs identified in UAT, ready for soft launch.
🚀
December 17, 2025
Soft Launch: 50–100 applicants/day
Rolled out to new applicants only at controlled exposure. Monitored completion rates, Clarity recordings, and critical metrics. No production incidents on day one a testament to thorough testing.
📊
December 19, 2025
Initial Results: Inconclusive
After ~100 test users, control at 64.8% and treatment at 63% start-to-paid. Exposure still low, but payment screen identified as largest drop-off. Hypothesized need to set expectations earlier in flow.
🔧
January 2026
Post-Launch Refinement
Fixed remaining bugs (particularly CAR/TAR application issues). Began testing payment placement variants to understand if moving payment earlier/later impacted conversion and multi-share attach.
📈
February 19, 2026
Parity Experiment Design
Concluded: moving payment to end of flow actually widened the gap (treatment performed worse). Determined we needed a "Parity with Legacy" test to isolate UX variables from new architecture variables. Designed experiment targeting new users, 70% control / 30% test, 1-week duration.
🎓
March 2026
Launch: Parity with Legacy Experiment
Launched refined experiment to prove clean measurement and isolated variable testing. Set foundation for iterating on UX improvements in phase 2 (e.g., progress indicators, payment messaging, completion psychology).

Key Learnings

What We Discovered

Multi-Share Placement Matters
When we moved payment to the end of the flow, multi-share attach rate returned to parity with control. The legacy experience had applicants invest time before payment psychologically priming them for add-ons. Simple reordering revealed deep insights into user momentum.
Measurement Precision Required
Early experiments had data quality issues (users labeled in both control and test). We rebuilt experiment setup with stricter cohort definitions, cleaner bucketing, and stronger validation critical for future iterations.
Progress Indicators Drive Completion
The legacy "summary" page served as a progress checkpoint. Its removal in the redesign reduced perceived progress and user momentum. Phase 2 will re-introduce structured progress indicators tied to completion psychology.
Design-First Beats Sprint-Driven
By designing weeks ahead, we had time to iterate on hypotheses (payment placement, summary layout, etc.) before they shipped. This prevented wasted engineering cycles and enabled rapid pivots based on data.

1. Established Design Leadership Cadence
By securing dedicated design time ahead of engineering sprints, we've created a repeatable model for phase 2 and future initiatives. The design team is now empowered to iterate and discover on its own timeline, feeding validated concepts to engineering.
2. Built Data-Driven Experimentation Infrastructure
Established rigorous A/B testing practices, cohort definitions, and metrics infrastructure. The "Parity with Legacy" experiment framework will support phase 2 payment placement tests and multi-share variants with high confidence.
3. Unlocked Cleaner Code Architecture
Replaced legacy codebase with modern, maintainable code. This unblocks faster shipping, reduces technical debt, and enables designers and engineers to iterate on applicant experience improvements for years to come.
4. Clarified Product North Star
This initiative articulated a clear vision for applicant experience: mobile-first, linear, transparent, and frictionless. All future work on the application flow will measure against this north star.

Reflection

What's Next

Phase 1 proved the architectural shift was sound and established a foundation for rapid iteration. Phase 2 will focus on conversion optimization: refined payment placement, progressive disclosure of multi-share, enhanced progress indicators, and psychological cues that drive completion.

By embedding design leadership upstream of engineering designing weeks ahead, validating assumptions early, and measuring rigorously we've positioned the product design team as strategic partners in growth. This methodology will define how we approach product challenges across RentSpree for years to come.
