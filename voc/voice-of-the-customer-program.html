The Challenge

Customer Feedback Was Everywhere and Actionable Nowhere

RentSpree operates a multi-sided marketplace with three distinct user types: property reps, renters, and landlords. Each had different pain points and communicated through different channels. Support tickets arrived through HubSpot, call recordings through Aircall, satisfaction scores through Survicate, and public reviews through Trustpilot each owned by a different team, siloed in a different tool, and disconnected from product decisions. There was no unified system to surface patterns, no mechanism to route insights to product, and no way to measure whether things were improving over time.

🔇

Fragmented Feedback Channels

Survey responses, call transcripts, support tickets, and public reviews all lived in separate systems with no integration layer. Finding the signal required manual, time-intensive effort across multiple platforms.

🔁

No Closed Feedback Loop

Issues surfaced by customers would resolve in the support queue without ever reaching product. There was no systematic path from "customer complaint" to "product consideration" pain points got lost in transition.

📊

No Baseline or Trend Tracking

Without structured metric tracking, the business had no reliable way to know whether customer satisfaction was improving or declining making it impossible to tie product changes to customer outcomes.

🎯

Unclear Signal vs. Noise

With no frequency analysis or aggregation, individual complaints and recurring pain points looked identical in priority. Product and operations had no data-driven way to separate what needed fixing from what was an edge case.

The Framework

A Continuous Feedback Engine, Not a One-Time Research Project

The VOC program was designed as a living system one that ingests feedback continuously, analyzes it automatically, and distributes insights to every part of the company on a regular cadence. The goal was not just to understand customers in a given quarter, but to make customer feedback a permanent input to how RentSpree prioritizes, builds, and operates.

At its core, the program connects three feedback sources Survicate (post-transaction surveys), AircallAI (call transcripts), and HubSpot (support tickets) to a centralized BigQuery data warehouse. NLP models run sentiment scoring and topic extraction automatically. Insights flow outward in three directions: real-time dashboards, automated monthly AI summaries posted to Slack, and a monthly VOC newsletter distributed company-wide.

"The goal was to make customer feedback impossible to ignore built into the rhythm of the company, not locked in a research report."

Head of Product Design and Research, RentSpree

The Methodology

From Raw Feedback to Roadmap-Ready Insights

The program operates on a six-stage loop running continuously since November 2023. Each stage has a clear owner, a defined output, and a handoff point ensuring customer feedback doesn't stall anywhere on its journey from collection to action.

Capture → Ingest → Analyze → Distribute → Act → Measure → (repeat)
The VOC continuous improvement loop bi-weekly cadence, running since November 2023
  • 1

    Capture

    Feedback arrives from three automated sources: Survicate post-transaction surveys measuring CSAT at key moments in the rental journey, AircallAI transcribing and summarizing customer phone calls, and HubSpot logging and categorizing support ticket topics. Each source covers different user types and touchpoints, together providing a 360° view of the customer experience.

  • 2

    Ingest & Normalize

    A BigQuery data pipeline ingests, deduplicates, and normalizes raw data from all three sources into a Consolidated Feedback Table the single source of truth for all VOC analysis. This step strips source-specific formatting inconsistencies and aligns all data into a unified schema, enabling cross-source comparisons.

  • 3

    Analyze

    NLP models run automatically on the consolidated data, performing sentiment scoring and topic extraction. The output surfaces recurring themes (e.g., "application editing friction," "rent payment errors," "ID verification delays"), assigns sentiment polarity, and tracks frequency trends across time periods and user segments.

  • 4

    Distribute

    Insights are distributed through three channels: live dashboards accessible to all stakeholders at any time, a monthly AI-generated summary posted automatically to the #voice-of-the-customer Slack channel, and a curated monthly VOC newsletter distributed to the entire company covering key findings and emerging themes.

  • 5

    Act Quick Wins Framework

    The bi-weekly VOC meeting brings together Customer Operations, Product, and cross-functional stakeholders to review the latest insights. The meeting is structured to end with identified Quick Wins product-actionable fixes with high customer impact and lower implementation cost each assigned to a specific team with supporting VOC data as the business case.

  • 6

    Measure

    CSAT scores, Trustpilot ratings, ticket volume by category, and topic frequency are tracked over time. This measurement layer closes the loop: validating whether VOC-driven changes are moving the metrics, and informing where to direct attention in the next cycle.

Rollout & Evolution

Two Years of Building, Iterating, and Scaling

The VOC program didn't launch fully-formed. It grew from a simple bi-weekly meeting with manual feedback review into a multi-channel insight engine with automated data pipelines, AI-generated summaries, and a formal cross-functional operating structure adding capability at each stage.

🌱
November 2023
Program Founded
The first bi-weekly VOC meeting is held, bringing together the Customer Operations Lead, Data Team Lead, and Product stakeholders. Initial scope: gather, review, and discuss customer feedback on a structured cadence the first time this had happened systematically at RentSpree.
🗄️
Early 2024
BigQuery Pipeline Built
The Data Team builds the first version of the VOC data pipeline, connecting Survicate and HubSpot/AircallAI to BigQuery. The Consolidated Feedback Table is established as the single source of truth for all customer insight analysis replacing manual, one-off data pulls.
📰
2024 (Ongoing)
Monthly VOC Newsletter Launched
A monthly VOC newsletter begins distribution company-wide, distilling the most important customer insights, sentiment trends, and emerging themes for all teams including Product, Marketing, Engineering, and Leadership. This is the program's first step toward building a shared customer vocabulary across the organization.
🎫
Early 2025
HubSpot Ticket Source Added to Pipeline
The data pipeline expands to include structured HubSpot ticket data, significantly enriching the available signal for NLP analysis. This addition enables category-level ticket frequency tracking across all user segments making the "what are customers asking for help with most?" question answerable with data for the first time.
💬
April 2025
Dedicated Slack Channel Created
The #voice-of-the-customer Slack channel is created, giving the program a permanent async home. Cross-functional contributors can share insights, discuss themes, and follow up on Quick Wins between bi-weekly meetings reducing dependency on the synchronous meeting as the only collaboration surface.
🤖
Mid 2025
AI Data Insight Bot Deployed
An automated AI bot begins posting monthly data insight summaries directly to #voice-of-the-customer, synthesizing BigQuery pipeline output into readable narratives. This removes the manual reporting step that had previously required significant Data Team time, and increases the freshness and accessibility of insights for the entire company.
📋
Late 2025
Survey Revamp Completed
Survicate surveys are redesigned to capture richer, more structured signal across all three user segments. The revamp improves response quality, segment tagging accuracy, and the reliability of CSAT scores as leading indicators of customer health making the satisfaction metrics more comparable across time periods.
⚡
2025–2026
Quick Wins Framework Formalized
Customer Operations formalizes the Quick Wins process a structured approach to identifying high-frequency, high-impact product issues from VOC data and routing them to Product with supporting evidence. Multiple product fixes ship directly as a result, including the "edit application" fix, the account merge resolution, and the apply link repair each demonstrating VOC's ability to close the product feedback loop.

Organizational reach

The Impact

Customer Obsession Took Root Across the Organization

Over two-plus years of continuous operation, the VOC program achieved its primary goal: making customer obsession a company-wide default, not a specialist function. Every team at RentSpree now has access to the same customer signal, speaks the same vocabulary around pain points, and participates in the same feedback loop. The measurable outcomes are a by-product of that cultural shift.

Quick Wins Became Systematic Output
The "edit application" fix (7.6% of all tickets), account merge resolution (previously ~20% of support volume), and apply link repair (500+ affected users) were all identified through VOC data not through product intuition or executive escalations. Each was surfaced by ticket frequency analysis, validated by customer sentiment, and routed to engineering with a data-backed case for prioritization.
AI Automation Reduced Reporting Overhead
The monthly AI-generated summary bot eliminated the manual step of synthesizing feedback data for each newsletter cycle. By automating the first draft of insight narratives from BigQuery output, the Data Team was freed to focus on deeper analysis, pipeline improvements, and new data source integrations rather than time-consuming report writing.
Shared Customer Vocabulary Across Teams
The monthly newsletter created a common language around customer pain points that now spans Product, Operations, Marketing, and Leadership. Teams that previously had no visibility into support ticket patterns reference VOC themes when making decisions about features, messaging, and support investments customer empathy spread from a specialist function to a company-wide default.
Foundation for an Ongoing Feedback Culture
Two-plus years of continuous operation established the VOC program as infrastructure not a project. The bi-weekly cadence, the Slack channel, the newsletter, and the automated data pipeline are now default parts of how RentSpree listens to and acts on customer signal, embedded in the operating rhythm of Customer Operations, Product, and the Data Team alike.
