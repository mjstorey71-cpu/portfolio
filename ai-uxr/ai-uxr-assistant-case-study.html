The Challenge

Research insights existed - but no one could find them

RentSpree's UX Research team had conducted over 90 research studies since 2022. But those reports lived in a Notion repository that was difficult to navigate, time-consuming to search, and largely unknown to teams outside of design. Valuable insights were being rediscovered from scratch, decisions were made without research backing, and the small UXR team was constantly fielding ad-hoc requests just to surface information that already existed.

🔍

Invisible Knowledge

90+ research reports existed in Notion, but discovering the right study required knowing where to look, what to search for, and how the reports were organized - a skill only the UXR team reliably had.

🔂

Constant Re-Discovery

Without an accessible repository, teams often repeated research questions that had already been answered - wasting researcher time and delaying decisions that could have been made with existing evidence.

⏳

UXR Team Bottleneck

The UXR team received frequent pings asking "do we have research on X?" - pulling them away from active research work and creating a dependency that didn't scale as the company grew.

📉

Decaying Research Value

Research studies degrade in utility the harder they are to find. Insights that could inform product decisions, roadmap prioritization, and cross-team alignment simply weren't being used.

The Framework

A research repository that talks back

The vision was straightforward: build an AI-powered assistant where anyone at RentSpree could ask a plain-English question - "what do users think about the listing experience?" or "has there been research on landlord onboarding?" - and receive a cited, research-backed answer in seconds.

Rather than replacing the UXR team, this tool would amplify their work. Every study they conducted would become a permanent, searchable, queryable asset - available to product managers, engineers, designers, marketing, and leadership alike, without any mediation required. The UXR team would be freed to focus on generating new insights rather than serving as a search engine for old ones.

Critically, every response would include citations pointing to specific source documents - so users could trust the output, verify claims directly, and develop their own fluency with the research repository over time.

The Methodology

From idea to company-wide launch in three months

This project ran as a structured POC-to-production initiative, moving through problem definition, build, evaluation, and launch in rapid succession. The approach was deliberately iterative: build a working system early, test it rigorously, then make a data-driven decision on the best path forward.

Research Archive → AI-Ready Docs → Vector Embeddings → Semantic Retrieval → Cited Answer
The core pipeline: structured research + AI retrieval = accessible, trustworthy insights
  • 1

    Define the problem & write the PRD

    Mapped the current state of research access at RentSpree: 90+ reports in Notion, no AI-readable structure, and a UXR team fielding constant "do we have research on X?" requests. Defined the objective as making the repository self-serve and instant - tied directly to the 2026 OKR of creating a curated research repository tool.

  • 2

    Build the custom RAG pipeline

    Engineered a Retrieval-Augmented Generation system on Google Cloud Run using Vertex AI Discovery API, OpenAI vector store, and LangChain for short-term conversational memory. The pipeline converts research documents into embeddings, enabling semantic search across the entire archive. Hosted on GCP at approximately $5–10/month in infrastructure cost.

  • 3

    Curate and tag the knowledge base

    Worked with the UX Research team to process and standardize all 90+ research reports into AI-ready format. This involved tagging studies by topic, participant type, and research method - ensuring the AI could retrieve and contextualize findings accurately. The resulting ai-ready-uxr-docs repository became the canonical knowledge source.

  • 4

    Run a rigorous benchmarking test

    To make an objective build-vs-buy decision, a 60-question benchmark was developed covering a range of topic areas and query types. Both the custom RAG AI and Google's NotebookLM were evaluated head-to-head. Metrics included accuracy score, citation precision, average response time, and ability to handle ambiguous or complex queries. The results informed the final beta launch decision.

  • 5

    Launch beta to all of RentSpree

    Based on benchmarking results and resource sustainability considerations, NotebookLM was selected for the beta launch - offering strong accuracy with zero maintenance overhead. The RentSpree UXR Notebook launched company-wide on February 18, 2026, accessible via a shared link and through a Slack bot integration (@UX Research AI). The custom RAG system remains the foundation for future production deployment and now powers the company's broader AI source-of-truth infrastructure.

The Benchmarking

A data-driven decision between two capable systems

Rather than defaulting to either building from scratch or adopting an off-the-shelf solution, both approaches were tested head-to-head against a standardized 60-question benchmark. Every question was evaluated for accuracy, citation quality, and response speed - giving clear, comparable data to inform the launch decision.

The Rollout

From POC to company-wide beta in three months

The project moved quickly from a clearly defined problem to a working POC, then through technical build and rigorous evaluation before reaching a full company launch in February 2026 - all in under three months.

🚀
September 2025
Project Kickoff & PRD
The project was formally kicked off as part of the 2026 departmental OKR initiative. The Product Requirements Document was written, defining the problem space, proposed AI workflow, and success criteria. POC development began immediately.
⚙️
October 2025
Custom RAG Pipeline Built
The custom Retrieval-Augmented Generation pipeline was engineered on Google Cloud Run. Core components included Vertex AI Discovery API for document retrieval, OpenAI vector store for semantic embeddings, and LangChain for short-term conversational memory. Initial integration and testing began with a subset of the research library.
🎯
November 2025
RAG Server v1.0 Released
The first complete version of the custom RAG AI was released. The full UXR workflow was operational end-to-end - from document ingestion to natural language query and cited response generation. Internal testing began with the design and research team.
📚
January 2026
Knowledge Base Complete
The UX Research team completed curation and tagging of all 90+ research reports into AI-ready format. The ai-ready-uxr-docs repository was finalized, establishing the canonical knowledge source. All documents were structured for reliable vector embedding and retrieval.
📊
February 2026
Benchmarking & Launch Decision
A 60-question benchmark was run head-to-head comparing the custom RAG AI against Google NotebookLM. Results showed RAG at 91% accuracy (4s avg) vs NotebookLM at 80.8% (10s avg) - both at 100% citation precision. Leadership chose NotebookLM for the beta launch based on zero maintenance overhead and sustainability, while the custom RAG became the backbone of the company's broader AI source-of-truth infrastructure.
🎉
February 18, 2026
Company-Wide Beta Launch
The RentSpree UXR Notebook launched to all of RentSpree - accessible via a shared NotebookLM link and through the @UX Research AI Slack bot. The launch generated immediate enthusiasm, with product managers and marketing teams integrating the tool into their daily workflows within days of going live.

What was once a niche resource requiring specialized navigation became an always-on tool used daily by product managers and marketing - and is now the foundation feeding other AI systems across the organization.
