The Challenge

Stuck in the Build Trap

When I joined RentSpree post-Series B, the company was scaling fast but the product and design organization had not kept pace. MVP features were being launched without validating product-market fit or understanding customer needs. The result was poor adoption, inconsistent UI, and a team locked in analysis paralysis. The collective product development teams worked in silos and were not making meaningful impact.

🔀

Fragmented Experience

Inconsistent UI across product areas created cognitive overload. The back end had effectively been shipped to the customer with no real hierarchy, guidance, or help content integrated.

📦

Output Over Outcomes

Teams were shipping features without solving real problems. Decisions were driven by velocity targets rather than customer insight, accumulating UX debt with each release.

😰

Customers Overwhelmed

Confusing navigation, cluttered content, and a lack of action focus meant that even core workflows required significant effort from users, eroding trust and conversion.

🌱

Team Without Direction

A junior team with no established mentorship structure, no shared design language, and no framework for connecting design decisions to business outcomes.

"We were stuck in the build trap, shipping features without solving real problems. The result was UX debt, cognitive overload, and missed opportunity."

- Michael Storey, Head of Product Design and Research

The Strategy

From Feature Factory to Customer-Obsessed Machine

The transformation required simultaneous change across people, process, and product. The core idea was to rebuild the operating model from the ground up: embedding design into product trios, shifting to continuous discovery, and creating a vision-driven planning cadence that connected design metrics to business goals.

Critically, this was not just a design team change. Design leaders were embedded early to shape company culture, and the language of design was translated into business terms that resonated with the C-suite and engineering partners alike. Moving from a point-solution approach to a guided, journey-based product experience required rethinking both the IA and the modular design system that underpinned it.

The Process

A Systematic Approach to Transformation

The transformation was sequenced deliberately: diagnose, align, redesign, validate, and scale. Each phase built on the last, ensuring the team developed both capability and confidence before taking on larger challenges.

Discovery + Strategy + System Thinking = Customer Obsession at Scale
The operating model that turned design from a service function into a strategic driver
  • 1

    Diagnose and Listen

    Conducted deep stakeholder discovery to understand business problems, team dynamics, and the existing product's pain points. Mapped the legacy experience to identify UX debt and architectural confusion before proposing solutions.

  • 2

    Rebuild the Team and Operating Model

    Restructured the design org using a hybrid model with clear roles. Introduced multidisciplinary skill sets and hired senior talent to raise the capability bar. Embedded designers as co-owners of product squads, not just execution partners.

  • 3

    Establish Discovery and Experimentation

    Introduced A/B testing and a continuous discovery cadence. Ran moderated usability studies with close PM and engineering involvement to build cross-functional empathy and shared ownership of the product experience.

  • 4

    Design and Build the New System

    Led concept sprints where the team broke into groups to explore visual and architectural directions. Developed a modular design system and brand identity that brought warmth, clarity, and relationship-focused storytelling to the product.

  • 5

    Shift to Journey-Based Experience

    Mapped customer mental models and jobs to be done, which formed the basis of core product objects and navigation. Cut low-adoption features, introduced guided workflows, and moved to a mobile-first design approach.

  • 6

    Measure, Iterate, and Scale

    Validated changes through ongoing research and quantitative metrics. Iterated on high-impact areas such as the marketing site, application flow, and landlord experience based on direct data. Built a culture of continuous improvement that outlasted the initial transformation sprint.

The Timeline

Eighteen Months of Deliberate Change

The transformation unfolded in phases, each building capability and confidence before taking on the next challenge. The pace was fast, but never at the expense of quality or team wellbeing.

🔍
Q3 2022
Discovery and Diagnosis
Joined RentSpree as Head of Product Design and Research. Conducted a comprehensive audit of the product, team, and organizational design. Identified four core gaps: fragmented UI, output-over-outcome culture, junior team without mentorship, and customers overwhelmed by complexity.
🏗
Q4 2022
Team Rebuild and Process Design
Restructured the design organization using a hybrid model. Introduced weekly discovery rituals, stakeholder alignment sessions, and a shared framework for connecting design decisions to business outcomes. Hired senior talent to raise the bar.
🎨
Q1 2023
Brand and Design System Overhaul
Led collaborative concept sprints producing multiple visual directions. Developed a modular design system, brand identity with photography and illustration guidelines, tone of voice, color palette, and typography. Team ownership was by design.
🗺
Q2 to Q3 2023
Journey Mapping and IA Redesign
Conducted customer research to map mental models and jobs to be done. Redesigned the information architecture to reflect how customers actually think. Cut low-adoption features and introduced journey-based navigation. Moved the team to mobile-first.
🚀
Q4 2023
Product Launch and A/B Validation
Shipped the redesigned marketing site and core product experience. CTA conversion increased 17% immediately after launch. Moderated usability studies confirmed improved comprehension and task completion. A/B testing infrastructure enabled rapid iteration.
📈
Q1 2024
Scale and Recognition
Active users grew from 1.4M to 3.2M. Lease e-sign completion rose from 18% to 27%. Support call volume dropped 30%. Design was recognized as a strategic pillar. RentSpree was named a HousingWire Real Estate Tech100 honoree.
